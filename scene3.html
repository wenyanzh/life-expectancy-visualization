To summarize, from the charts, we can see that there is a positive relationship between life expectancy and GDP per capita. This is reasonable since GDP per capita increases the life expectancy through increasing economic growth and development in a country and thus leads to the prolongation of longevity. There is also a positive relationship between life expectancy and health expenditure per capita. This is reasonable as An increase in total health expenditure has a significant positive impact on health outcomes, which leads to a positive relationship between healthcare expenditures and life expectancy.
